Algebra — Polynomials & Factorization

Important identities
(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2
(a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2
(a + b)(a - b) = a^2 - b^2
a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2)
a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2)

Linear Equations

General form & solving
ax + by = c — represents a straight line in two variables
Elimination method: multiply and subtract to eliminate one variable
Substitution: express one variable and substitute into another equation

Geometry — Triangles & Pythagoras

Right triangle
For right triangle: a^2 + b^2 = c^2 (c = hypotenuse)
Area of triangle
Area = (1/2) × base × height
Similarity & Congruence
If corresponding angles equal and sides proportional → similar triangles

Mensuration — Areas & Perimeters

Rectangle: Area = l × b, Perimeter = 2(l + b)
Square: Area = a^2, Perimeter = 4a
Parallelogram: Area = base × height
Trapezium: Area = (1/2) × (sum of parallel sides) × height
Circle: Area = πr^2, Circumference = 2πr (π ≈ 22/7 or 3.1416)

Surface Area & Volume

Cuboid: Volume = l × b × h, Surface area = 2(lb + bh + hl)
Cube: Volume = a^3, Surface area = 6a^2
Cylinder: Volume = πr^2h, Total surface area = 2πr(h + r)
Cone (right): Volume = (1/3)πr^2h, TSA = πr(l + r) where l = slant height
Sphere: Volume = (4/3)πr^3, Surface area = 4πr^2

Angles — Parallel Lines & Transversals

Corresponding angles are equal
Alternate interior angles are equal
Co-interior (consecutive interior) angles are supplementary (sum = 180°)

Numbers, Powers & Indices

Laws of exponents: a^m × a^n = a^{m+n}, (a^m)^n = a^{mn}, a^0 = 1 (a ≠ 0)
Square root: (√a)^2 = a
If H = HCF and L = LCM of two numbers a and b, then a × b = H × L

Data Handling & Probability

Mean (arithmetic) = (sum of observations) / (number of observations)
Median = middle value after sorting (or average of two middle values)
Mode = most frequently occurring value
Probability of an event = favourable outcomes / total possible outcomes

Coordinate Geometry

Distance between (x1,y1) and (x2,y2): √[(x2-x1)^2 + (y2-y1)^2]
Midpoint: ( (x1+x2)/2 , (y1+y2)/2 )
Slope (m) of line through two points = (y2-y1)/(x2-x1)

Exam Tips & Shortcuts

Common tricks
Check units in mensuration problems. Convert to same unit before calculating.
Always draw a neat diagram for geometry problems — label given lengths and angles.
For algebraic identities — practise expansion and factorisation with numbers first.
